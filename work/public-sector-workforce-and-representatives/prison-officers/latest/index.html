Prison officer workforce

Department:
Ministry of Justice
Last updated:
24 November 2016, see all updates
Source:
NOMS Annual Staff Equalities report
Area covered:
England and Wales
Time period:
2013 to 2016

The main facts and figures show that:

  • in 2016, fewer than 6% of prison officers in England and Wales came from an ethnic minority background (excluding White ethnic minorities); this compares with 14% of people in England and Wales that belong to an ethnic minority group (excluding White ethnic minorities)
  • since 2013, Black and Other ethnic prison officers have seen the greatest reduction in their workforce, which have fallen by 44.2% and 43.2% respectively
Things you need to know

Between 31 March 2013 and 31 March 2016, the total number of prison officers employed in England and Wales fell by more than a third, to 19,089.

Prison officers give details of their ethnicity to an online administration system. However, they are not required to state their ethnicity, and many do not. In 2016, 16,448 stated their ethnicity

The percentages in the statistics presented here exclude prison officers whose ethnicities were not known. This adds a fairly high degree of uncertainty to the breakdowns by ethnicity.

If most of those prison officers not stating their ethnicity were in fact from an ethnic minority background, for example, the real percentage of ethnic minority prison officers would be higher than stated here. Conversely, if most prison officers who didn’t state their ethnicity were White, the percentage of ethnic minority prison officers would be lower than the figure indicated in this measure.

As there’s no way of knowing the ethnicity of those who didn’t state theirs, the data isn’t reliable enough to make a completely detailed or accurate assessment of the ethnic breakdown of the prison officer workforce.

Observations on ethnicity based on this data should be treated with caution. This is because the percentage of prison officers who didn’t state their ethnicity is considerably larger than the percentage of prison officers from an ethnic minority (not including White ethnic minorities).

What the data measures

The data measures the number of full-time and part-time prison officers in England and Wales, broken down by ethnicity.

It includes prison officers in salary bands 3 to 5, which cover frontline prison officers and custodial managers. It is a ‘snapshot’ of the number of prison officers employed on 31 March each year.

Why these ethnic categories were chosen

Prison officers were asked to place themselves within the following 5 broad ethnic categories:

  • Asian/Asian British
  • Black/African/Caribbean/Black British
  • Chinese or Other ethnic group
  • Mixed/Multiple ethnic groups
  • White

Summary

  • in March 2016, of prison officers whose ethnicities were known, 94.4% were White (including White ethnic minorities)
  • 5.7% of prison officers who did state their ethnicity were from an ethnic minority group (other than White ethnic minorities), roughly the same as one year earlier
  • 16.1% of prison officers didn’t state their ethnicity; this is an increase from about 10% the previous year

Methodology and type of data

Type of data

Administrative

Purpose of data source

Administrative data used for internal management information

Methodology

Data is derived from the staff administration system used by the National Offender Management Service. The numbers represent the data held at the point when extracts were taken. As with all large administrative data sources, some inherent inaccuracy may exist.

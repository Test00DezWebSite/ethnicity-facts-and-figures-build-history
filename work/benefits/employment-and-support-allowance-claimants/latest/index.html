People claiming Employment and Support Allowance

Department:
Department for Work and Pensions
Last updated:
01 October 2017, see all updates
Source:
Nomis
Area covered:
England and Wales
Time period:
2011 to 2015

The main facts and figures show that:

  • in 2015, across all regions of England and Wales except London, the percentage of White people claiming Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) was greater than the percentage of people from all Other ethnic groups claiming ESA

  • from 2011 to 2015, the percentage of people claiming ESA increased each year for both White people and people from all Other ethnic groups

  • the gap between the the percentage of White people and people from Other ethnic groups claiming ESA increased each year from 2011 to 2015, and was 1 percentage point in 2015

Things you need to know

The population figures used for this data are estimates based on the 2011 Census held in England and Wales.

The Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) claimant figures are based on Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) administrative data. They are the average number of claimants per month in each year.

The ethnicity questionnaires given to ESA claimants and in the 2011 Census are different, and the questions were answered at different times. As the two data sources don’t collect ethnicity information in the same way, the figures are broken down by White and all Other ethnic groups, but cannot be split down in any further detail.

The 2011 Census does not include an option for people who prefer not to state their ethnicity, while ESA claimants can choose not to do so. As a result, the figures shown for White and all Other ethnic groups may be lower than the actual totals because figures for unreported ethnicity are not included in those groups. In 2015, ethnicity was unreported for 27% of ESA claimants.

Figures for All ethnic groups do include ESA claimants whose ethnicity is unreported, so the sum of White and all Other ethnic groups does not equal the total for All claimants.

What the data measures

This data measures the number of people who claim Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) as a percentage of the total working-age population.

ESA supports people of working age (16 to 64 years) whose disability or health condition affects their ability to work. It offers financial support if they are unable to work and personalised help so that they can work if they’re able to.

Depending on the type of ESA, there are certain conditions that a claimant must meet before they can get the benefit.

The figures come from Department for Work and Pensions administrative data and population estimates based on the 2011 Census held in England and Wales.

Why these ethnic categories were chosen

Two different sources were used for these figures: Department for Work and Pensions administrative data and population estimates based on the 2011 Census. These data sources don’t collect ethnicity information in the same way and, as a result, the figures cannot be split into detailed ethnic categories.

Therefore, the data is broken down into the following 2 broad groups:

  • White – White ethnic groups, including White British and White ethnic minorities
  • all Other ethnic groups – all other ethnic minorities

People whose ethnicity is ‘Unreported’ are counted in measurements for ‘all’ groups, such as all people who are claiming Employment and Support Allowance, and not counted where data is broken down by White compared with all Other ethnic groups.

Methodology and type of data

Type of data

Administrative data and Census data

Purpose of data source

Employment and Support Allowance claimant count is administrative data used for internal management information.

Population counts are collected for the England and Wales Census held every 10 years.

Methodology

The population counts (the denominators) are estimates based on the 2011 England and Wales Census.

Claimant figures are the average number of claimants per month in each year.

The number of people claiming ESA has been drawn from administrative datasets held by the Department for Work and Pensions, which contain information on individuals’ benefit histories. The data is at the individual level, and individuals in receipt of the relevant benefit appear on that benefit’s dataset. These datasets are updated quarterly. This dataset contains information on all cases where payment was made to the claimant, and excludes those who have had their payment suspended, for example if they are in hospital.

The statistics cannot easily identify ESA claims of short duration. The ESA dataset is scanned every 2 weeks. This means that any claim that both starts and ends within the 2 weeks when data extracts are taken will not be picked up as part of the overall ESA data.

Claimant counts are also published on Nomis . Figures on Nomis are rounded, so differ slightly from those published here.

Rounding

Employment and Support Allowance claimant counts are rounded to the nearest 5. Percentages are rounded to the nearest 0.1%.

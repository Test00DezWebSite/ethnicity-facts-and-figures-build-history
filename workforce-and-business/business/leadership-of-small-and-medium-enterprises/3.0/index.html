1. Main facts and figures

  • in 2019, 5.1% of UK small and medium enterprise (SME) employers were led by a majority of people from an ethnic minority (excluding White minorities)
  • the health sector had the highest percentage of SME employers that were majority-led by ethnic minorities (8.2%)
  • the agriculture and utilities sectors had the lowest percentage of SME employers that were majority-led by ethnic minorities (0.1%)
  • Northern Ireland had the highest percentage of White-led SME employers out of all UK countries (90.7%)
  • England had the highest percentage of SME employers (5.4%) and non-employer SMEs (4.0%) that were majority-led by ethnic minorities
  • in 2019, 3.8% of UK SMEs with no employees were majority-led by those from an ethnic minority
  • the transport and storage industries had the lowest percentage of non-employer SMEs that were majority-led by ethnic minorities (0.4%)

2. Things you need to know

What the data measures

The data shows the percentage of UK small and medium enterprises (SMEs) that are led by a majority of people from:

  • an ethnic minority (excluding White minorities)
  • the White ethnic group

Businesses are defined as â€˜ethnic minority-ledâ€™ if 50% or more of their management team are from ethnic minorities, or if a person from an ethnic minority is in sole control of the business.

Data is shown for:

  • SME employers â€“ those with between one and 249 employees
  • SME non-employers â€“ those with no employees, and unregistered businesses

Percentages are rounded to 1 decimal place, so some totals may not add up to 100%.

Not included in the data

The data does not cover businesses with 250 or more employees, or businesses in the public sector.

Businesses that are not majority-led by people from an ethnic minority may still have some leaders from an ethnic minority background, but this is not shown in the figures.

The ethnic groups used in the data

Data is shown for 2 aggregated ethnic groups:

  • White â€“ including White British and White ethnic minorities
  • Other â€“ all other ethnic groups

This is because the number of people surveyed was too small to make any reliable conclusions about any of the 18 ethnic groups or 5 aggregated groups.

Ethnicity was unknown for 9.3% of SME employers and 8.5% of SME non-employers.

Methodology

Read the detailed methodology document for the data on this page.

In the data file

The figures in the download file may differ from those published in the Small Business Survey due to revisions made every year.

Summary of Leadership of small and medium enterprises By ethnicity and area (SMEs with employees) Summary

The data shows that in 2019:

  • 5.1% of UK small and medium enterprise (SME) employers were majority-led by ethnic minorities (excluding White minorities)
  • 85.6% of SME employers were majority-led by White people, and 9.3% had leadership teams whose ethnicity was unknown
  • SME employers that were majority-led by ethnic minorities were more common in England (5.4%) and Scotland (4.3%) than in Northern Ireland (2.1%) or Wales (1.5%)

Summary of Leadership of small and medium enterprises By ethnicity and area (SMEs without employees) Summary

The data shows that:

  • 3.8% of UK SMEs with no employees were majority-led by people from an ethnic minority (excluding White minorities)
  • 87.8% of SME non-employers were majority-led by White people, and 8.5% had leadership teams where ethnicity was unknown

  • SME non-employers that were majority-led by White people were more common in Wales (91.2%) than in England (87.8%), Northern Ireland (86.8%) or Scotland (85.6%)

Summary of Leadership of small and medium enterprises By ethnicity and sector (SMEs with employees) Summary

The data shows that:

  • SME employers that were majority-led by ethnic minorities were most common in health (8.2%), and information and communication (7.9%)
  • they were least common in agriculture and utilities (0.1%), and manufacturing (2.8%)
  • SME employers that were majority-led by White people were most common in agriculture and utilities (94.4%), manufacturing (93.6%), construction (91.6%), and transport and storage (91.1%)
  • ethnicity was unknown for a large percentage of businesses in the education (22.1%), health and social care (29.0%), arts and entertainment (22.1%), and accommodation and food sectors (10.5%)

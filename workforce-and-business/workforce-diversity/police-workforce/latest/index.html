1. Main facts and figures

  • at the end of March 2020, 92.7% of police officers were White and 7.3% were from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other ethnic backgrounds

  • 4.3% of senior officers were from the Asian, Black, Mixed and Other ethnic groups combined, compared with 2.8% in 2007

  • between 2007 and 2020, the percentage of police officers from every ethnic minority group went up â€“ the biggest increase was for Asian police officers (from 1.5 to 3.1%)

  • at the time of the 2011 Census, 86.0% of people in England and Wales were White and 14.0% were from other ethnic groups

2. Things you need to know

What the data measures

The data shows the number and percentage of people working for the police who come from different ethnic groups. It covers the 43 police forces in England and Wales, and the British Transport Police.

The data comes from a snapshot taken on 31 March every year.

Percentages are based on 'full-time equivalent' (FTE) staff numbers. For example, someone who works 2.5 days a week has an FTE of 0.5.

Percentages are rounded to 1 decimal place.

The figures on this page are based on Home Office police workforce data â€“ this is where you can find the first release and most up-to-date data.

Not included in the data

The data does not include staff whose ethnicity was not known. In 2020, this was:

  • 2.6% of police officers
  • 3.4% of the total police workforce
  • 8.2% of special constables

The ethnic groups used in the data

Police officers and staff in England and Wales report their own ethnicity based on the following 5 aggregated ethnic groups:

  • Asian
  • Black
  • Mixed
  • White
  • Other ethnic group (including Chinese)

Methodology

Read the detailed methodology document (PDF opens in a new window or tab) for this data.

Population estimates come from the 2011 Census. You can read more about how we use Census data.

Summary of Police workforce By ethnicity (police officers) Summary

The data shows that:

  • White people made up 92.7% of police officers at the end of March 2020, and 86.0% of the general population at the time of the 2011 Census

  • Asian people made up 3.1% of police officers, and 6.8% of the general population

  • people with Mixed ethnicity made up 2.2% of police officers, and 2.2% of the general population

  • Black people made up 1.3% of police officers, and 3.3% of the general population

  • people from the Other ethnic group made up 0.8% of police officers, and 1.7% of the general population

Summary of Police workforce By ethnicity and area (police officers) Summary

The data shows that:

  • in 2020, the Metropolitan Police had the highest percentage of police officers from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other backgrounds combined (15.5%) out of all police forces

  • at the time of the 2011 Census, 40.2% of people in London were from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other ethnic backgrounds

  • in the West Midlands, 11.6% of police officers and 29.9% of the general population were from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other ethnic backgrounds

  • in Bedfordshire, 10.1% of police officers and 22.5% of the general population were from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other ethnic backgrounds

  • police forces in North Wales, Cumbria, and Dyfed-Powys had the smallest percentage of officers from Asian, Black, Mixed or Other backgrounds (at 0.9%, 1.0% and 1.3%).

â€˜Senior officersâ€™ include Chief Officers, Chief Superintendents, Superintendents and Chief Inspectors.

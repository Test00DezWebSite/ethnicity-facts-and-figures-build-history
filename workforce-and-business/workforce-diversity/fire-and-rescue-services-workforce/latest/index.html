1. Main facts and figures

  • in 2020, 95.6% of firefighters in England were White (out of those whose ethnicity was known)
  • for comparison, 85.1% of working age people in England (aged 16 to 64 years) were White at the time of the 2011 Census
  • from 2011 to 2020, the total number of firefighters went down from around 43,000 to around 35,000
  • there was a decrease in the number of firefighters in every ethnic group except the Mixed and Asian ethnic groups
  • overall, the percentage of fire and rescue services staff (including support staff) from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other backgrounds went up from 4.0% in 2011 to 5.0% in 2020

2. Things you need to know

What the data measures

The data shows the number and percentage of people working in fire and rescue services in England, by ethnicity. It is a headcount of all full-time and part-time staff employed on 31 March 2020.

Percentages are rounded to 1 decimal place. They are worked out using unrounded numbers. For this reason, some totals in the charts and tables may not add up to 100%.

The ethnic groups used in the data

Data is shown for the 5 ethnic groups used in the 2001 Census.

Percentages are based on staff whose ethnicity was known. Ethnicity was not known for 10% of staff in 2020.

Summary of Fire and rescue services workforce By ethnicity and type of role over time Summary

The data shows that, from 2011 to 2020:

  • the number of firefighters went down in every ethnic group except the Mixed and Asian groups
  • the number of White firefighters went down by 20%, from 38,061 to 30,298
  • the total number of firefighters from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other backgrounds went up by 2%, from 1,378 to 1,410
  • the percentage of firefighters who had Mixed ethnicity went up from 1.2% to 2.0% â€“ the biggest increase out of all ethnic groups
  • the percentage of fire and rescue staff in all roles from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other backgrounds went up from 4.0% to 5.0%
  • the percentage of support staff from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other backgrounds went up from 6.7% to 8.0%
  • the increase in the percentage of staff from Asian, Black and Other backgrounds is mainly down to the decrease in the number of White staff

Summary of Fire and rescue services workforce By type of role within each ethnic group Summary

The data shows that, in 2020:

  • 79.6% of all White staff and 84.1% of staff with Mixed ethnicity were firefighters
  • 51.4% of all Asian staff, 64.6% of Black staff, and 69.6% of staff from the Other (including Chinese) ethnic group were firefighters

  • staff from Black (34.0%) and Other (29.4%) backgrounds also had higher percentages in support staff roles than White staff

Summary of Fire and rescue services workforce By ethnicity and area (firefighters only) Summary

The diversity of the population in each area should be considered when looking at these figures. The areas served by each fire and rescue service do not exactly match the geographical areas covered by Census data.

The data shows that, in 2020:

  • Greater London had the highest percentage of firefighters from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other backgrounds (13.6%) out of all fire and rescue services
  • 40.2% of people in London were from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other backgrounds at the time of the 2011 Census
  • Cumbria (0.2%) and West Sussex, Northumberland, and Devon and Somerset (all 0.8%), had the smallest percentage of firefighters from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other backgrounds
  • 11.7% of firefighters in the West Midlands were from Asian, Black, Mixed and Other backgrounds

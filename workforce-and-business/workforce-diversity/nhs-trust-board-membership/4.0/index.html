1. Main facts and figures

New data as at 31 March 2021 is available if you download the data. The charts, tables and commentary on this page cover as at 31 March 2019 and do not include the new data.

As at 31 March 2019,:

  • 86.6% of the board members in NHS trusts were White (including White ethnic minorities) and 8.4% were from the Other ethnic group (all other ethnic groups combined); ethnicity wasnâ€™t known for 5.0%

  • NHS trusts in London had the highest percentage of board members from the Other ethnic group (17.1%), while trusts in the South West of England had the smallest percentage (3.0%)

  • more than half (67.8%) of NHS trusts had at least one board member from the Other ethnic group, and 73 out of 227 trust boards (32.2%) had none

  • 30 NHS trusts (13.1%) had 3 or more board members from the Other ethnic group.

Things you need to know

The data comes from all 227 NHS trusts. You should use caution when interpreting this data due to the small numbers of board members on NHS trusts.

In 2019, NHS trust boards ranged in size from 5 to 21 members, and the average board had 14 members. Therefore, small differences in composition between trusts can lead to large percentage differences.

Another result of these small numbers is that differences in the number of board members declaring their ethnicity can have a large impact on the percentage of members in each ethnic group for each trust.

For 19 trusts, 20% or more of their board members did not declare their ethnicity. For this reason, the data for each board includes the percentage of members for whom ethnicity was not known.

This data is based on the figures submitted to NHS England as at 31 March 2019. It does not incorporate any updates trusts may have made and published themselves.

What the data measures

This data measures the number and percentage of NHS trust board members from each of the following 2 broad ethnic groups:

  • White â€“ including White British and White ethnic minorities
  • Other â€“ all other ethnic groups combined, referred to as â€˜BME (Black and Minority Ethnic)â€™ in NHS England WRES reports

Percentages are calculated by dividing the number of board members from each broad ethnic group by the total number of board members, including those for whom ethnicity wasnâ€™t known. Ethnicity wasnâ€™t known for 5.0% of board members overall.

This data includes all board members, including â€˜non-executive membersâ€™ who are not part of the trustâ€™s management team, and members with no voting rights. Executive members of the board are usually at the â€˜Very Senior Managementâ€™ grade, for example, directors of specific functions like nursing or human resources.

The regions in England are grouped as follows:

East of England London Midlands North East and Yorkshire North West South East of England *South West of England

The ethnic categories used in this data

For this data, ethnicity was categorised into 2 broad ethnic groups to be consistent with the rest of the 2019 NHS Workforce Race Equality Standard report, and to increase the reliability of the data. This classification was also used for all previous WRES reports, so using the same classification allows for comparisons with previous years.

Summary of NHS trust board membership By ethnicity and trust Summary

This data shows that:

  • as at 31 March 2019, 73 NHS trusts (32.2%) had no board members from the Other ethnic group (which combines all ethnic groups except White); however, in 12 of these 73 trusts, 20% or more board members did not declare their ethnicity so this result should be treated with caution
  • the remaining 154 boards (67.7%) had between one and 8 members from the Other ethnic group, and 30 trusts (13.1%) had 3 or more members from the Other ethnic group
  • the East London NHS Foundation Trust had 9 board members from the Other ethnic group, the highest number of any board
  • East London NHS Foundation Trust had the highest percentage of board members from the Other ethnic group (50%)

4. Methodology

Data was collected in each NHS trust. Some trusts recorded board membership in the NHSâ€™s main HR and payroll system and others used internal databases. All trusts had to enter their data into the Strategic Data Collection System online collection system, which allows trusts to share their data with NHS England.

The data comes from all 227 NHS trusts.

Calculations of percentages includes members whose ethnicity was not known.

Rounding

Percentages are given to the nearest whole number.

1. Main facts and figures

  • from 2009 to 2016, the percentage of offenders given an immediate custodial sentence increased for White, Black, Asian and Mixed offenders

  • from 2009 to 2016, a lower percentage of White offenders were given an immediate custodial sentence than Black or Asian offenders

  • among juveniles, the Asian and Black ethnic groups had the highest percentage of offenders sent to a young offenders institution

  • out of all female offenders, Black offenders had the highest percentage given an immediate custodial sentence

  • Black offenders had the highest percentage given an immediate custodial sentence for drugs offences; White offenders had the highest percentage given an immediate custodial sentence for theft

Things you need to know

Where an offender was found guilty of more than one offence, the data includes the offence for which the offender was given the heaviest penalty. If they were given the same penalty for two or more offences, the data uses the offence with the heaviest statutory maximum penalty. Where more than one sentence is given for an offence, the most serious sentence is included in the data.

Ethnicity wasnâ€™t known or declared for about 1 in 5 offenders. This analysis only includes offenders for whom the ethnicity was known.

What the data measures

The data measures the percentage of offenders sentenced to immediate custody in court, as a percentage of all offenders sentenced for â€˜indictableâ€™ offences. We refer to this in places as the â€˜custody rateâ€™. The data is broken down by ethnicity, age and gender.

There are three broad types of offence:

  • indictable-only offences, such as murder, rape or robbery, which can only be heard in the Crown Court
  • offences that are triable either way, which means they can be heard in either a magistratesâ€™ court or in the Crown Court, and include offences like theft from shops and handling stolen goods
  • summary offences, such as speeding and TV licence evasion, which are heard in a magistratesâ€™ court

This data includes sentences for indictable-only offences and offences that are triable either way. We use the term â€˜indictable offenceâ€™ in this commentary to cover both types of offence.

Offenders are classified by age group as follows:

  • juveniles are aged under 18 years
  • young adults are aged between 18 and 20 years
  • adults are aged 21 years and over

The data focuses mainly on offenders given an immediate custodial sentence. It also includes information on other sentences like:

  • suspended sentence orders
  • community sentences
  • fines
  • absolute or conditional discharges
  • compensation orders

An extra category of sentence, â€˜Otherwise dealt withâ€™, includes offenders given less common sentences that donâ€™t fit into these groups, like spending one day in police cells or being given a guardianship order.

The ethnic categories used in this data

This data uses the following broad ethnic groups based on the 2001 census:

  • Asian/Asian British
  • Black/African/Caribbean/Black British
  • Mixed/Multiple ethnic groups
  • White
  • Other ethnic group (including Chinese)

There is also a â€˜not statedâ€™ category, made up of people whose ethnicity wasnâ€™t known or who chose not to declare it. This analysis excludes those in the â€˜not statedâ€™ category (about 1 in 5 offenders).

Summary of Sentences and custody Custody rate by ethnicity over time Summary

The â€˜custody rateâ€™ is the percentage of offenders given an immediate custodial sentence, out of all offenders being sentenced in court for indictable offences.

This data shows that:

  • from 2009 to 2016, the only ethnic group for whom the custody rate for indictable offences went down was the Chinese and Other group, from 40.0% in 2009 to 33.4% in 2016

  • in the same period, the overall custody rate for indictable offences went up by 5.9 percentage points, from 24.6% to 30.5%

  • the custody rate for indictable offences went up for White offenders (by 8.8 percentage points), Black offenders (by 2.2 percentage points), Asian offenders (by 5.6 percentage points) and offenders of Mixed ethnicity (by 8.4 percentage points)

  • White offenders sentenced for indictable offences had consistently lower custody rates than than Black or Asian offenders

This data shows that:

  • among all age and ethnicity groups, Asian adult offenders (aged 21 years and over) had the highest custody rate for indictable offences (at 37.2%)

  • among juveniles, Asian and Black offenders had the highest custody rates, at 13.1% and 11.9% respectively, compared to 9.2% for White juvenile offenders

  • White offenders had the lowest custody rates in all age groups

Summary of Sentences and custody Type of sentence by ethnicity of offender Summary

This data shows that:

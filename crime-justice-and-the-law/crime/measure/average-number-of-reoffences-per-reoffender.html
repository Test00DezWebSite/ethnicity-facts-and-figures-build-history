Average number of reoffences per criminal offender

Location:
England and Wales
Time period:
2000 to 2015
Source:

Department:
Ministry of Justice
Last updated:
24 November 2016

The main facts and figures show that:

  • White offenders had the highest average number of reoffences per offender for every year in the time period, and committed an average of 3.3 in 2015

  • all ethnic groups fell to their lowest average number of reoffences per reoffender in 2009, but since then the average number has increased for all groups

The reliability of the data

Measuring true reoffending is difficult. Official records are taken from either the police or courts, but they underestimate the true level of reoffending. This is because not all crimes are detected and sanctioned and not all crimes and sanctions are recorded on one central system. Other methods of measuring reoffending, such as self-report studies, are likely to also underestimate the rate.

Percentage changes may not add up due to rounding of raw figures.

What the data measures

This data measures the average number of reoffences committed by criminal offenders in different ethnic groups.

Each of these is expressed as a percentage, broken down by ethnicity, age and geographical area, for each calendar year 2000 to 2015.

Reoffences are only counted if they:

  • are committed within one year of the offender’s previous offence (or 18 months if the case is still progressing through the courts after a year)
  • lead to a court conviction, caution, reprimand or warning

The data source is the National Offender Management Service (NOMS) Offender Equalities Statistics Bulletin – supplementary table 15.2.

Why the categories of ethnicities used in this data were chosen

For this data, the number of people counted was too small to draw any firm conclusions about specific ethnic categories. Therefore, the data is broken down into the following broad groups:

  • White
  • Black
  • Asian
  • Other ethnic group

Purpose Of Data Source

Admin data used for internal management information

Methodology

A certain proportion of offenders who could not be matched to the Police National Computer (PNC) are excluded from the offender cohort. Therefore, these numbers do not represent all proven offenders. Admin data sources reported as recorded. As with all large administrative data sources, some inherent inaccuracy may exist.

Data type

Administrative data

Type of statistic

National statistic

Suppression rules

Proportions and averages based on less than 30 offenders are removed as they make data unreliable for interpretation. Number of reoffenders, reoffences and previous offences based on less than five offenders are suppressed. This is to prevent the disclosure of individual information.

Estimation

Rates as a percentage to 1dp
